In the ninth season, the differences in values were the greatest, but later we will see why this is so.
2. These three charts represent mean(), median(), standard deviation() for "Viewership" in each season.
{%include 'fig_mean_viewership.html'%}
{%include 'fig_median_viewership.html'%}
{%include 'fig_standard_deviation_viewership.html'%}
Overall, we can see that viewership changed a lot through the series. If we look at mean() we can draw a conclusion that the fifth season is the most viewed one. But as we will discover later, it happened like this because the most viewed episode in the entire series was released in this season (it can be understood if you look at the graph of "Standard Deviation"). So, in this case it's more appropriate to look at median(), and draw a conclusion that the third season is the most successful in this field. Considering the lowest viewership, we will arrive at a conclusion that it is the ninth season.

4. Charts representing the title of the episode that has the maximum and minimum score in each season.
{%include 'fig_highest.html'%}
{%include 'fig_lowest.html'%}
From this comparison we can understand that the eighth season is the worst one considering the rating of episode. It has the episode with the lowest rating among both the best and the worst - "Get the Girl". In contrast, we can conclude that the fifth season is the best. Even though it doesn't have the highest rated episode in this series, its lowest rated episode has "8.0". This means that all other episodes have a score from 8.0 to 9.5. So, the most interesting and well-writen episodes were released under this season. The first place in the score is shared by the episode from the seventh season called "Goodbye, Michael" and the final episode of the entire series "Finale" with score 9.8.
5. Chart representing the most and the least viewed season.
{%include 'fig_sum_of_views.html'%}
As a result, it can be seen that after the peak in fifth season with 227.67 million views in total the amount of views per each season began to decrease. In this case we can't analyse the fourth season because its number of episodes is less than most of the others. The first season has the least number of viewership but for the sake of cleanliness, it also has the smallest number of episodes. So, a conclusion can be drawn that the series reached its minimum at the final season.
6. Chart representing the most and the least viewed episodes in each season.
{%include 'fig_extremum_views.html'%}
So, as I mentioned before, we can't take into account the fifth season when calculating the average views of each season. "Stress Relief" has 22.91 million views and is more than twice exceeds the "Pilot" episode in this parameter. As we discovered earlier the eighth season can be considered as the worst in the whole series by rating. However, this does not prevent it from bypassing the last season in terms of both the most viewed episode and the least. The ninth season has the least viewership in this category. Episodes "Finale" and "Paper Airplane" have 5.69 and 3.25 million views respectively.
To sum up, the whole series was going pretty well. Its grades have always stayed around the same mark. But all this lasted until the eighth season. We can say that it became a turning point for the series. Big drops in ratings and views. If this season had not happened, then perhaps the performance of the ninth season would have been much better. It has good grades, but people no longer wanted to watch the series after a fairly passing eighth season.
